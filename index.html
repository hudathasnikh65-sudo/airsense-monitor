◈ FUTURE POLLUTION RISK PRECAUTIONS — LONG-TERM STRATEGY
🏗️
INFRASTRUCTURE
  • Install HEPA air purifiers at home and office
  • Use air quality sensors in every room
  • Seal window gaps with weather stripping
  • Plant air-purifying trees (Neem, Peepal) around home
  • Install green walls or vertical gardens
  • Switch to electric vehicles or public transport
🏥
HEALTH PREPAREDNESS
  • Get annual lung function tests (spirometry)
  • Maintain stock of N95/FFP2 masks
  • Carry bronchodilator inhaler if asthmatic
  • Take annual flu & pneumonia vaccines
  • Monitor blood pressure regularly
  • Build cardiovascular fitness to improve resilience
🌿
LIFESTYLE CHANGES
  • Shift outdoor exercise to early morning (5–7AM)
  • Avoid burning trash, crop stubble, firecrackers
  • Use LPG/induction instead of wood-fire cooking
  • Choose low-VOC paints and cleaning products
  • Eat antioxidant-rich foods (berries, turmeric, greens)
  • Stay hydrated — 2.5–3L water daily
🌍
COMMUNITY ACTION
  • Report illegal burning or industrial discharge
  • Plant 10 trees per household per year
  • Advocate for clean energy in local governance
  • Join neighbourhood air quality monitoring groups
  • Educate children on pollution and its effects
  • Support sustainable agriculture practices
🧪
DIETARY SHIELDS
  • Vitamin C (500mg/day) — antioxidant protection
  • Omega-3 fatty acids reduce lung inflammation
  • Turmeric + black pepper — anti-inflammatory
  • Ginger tea helps clear respiratory mucus
  • Avoid processed foods during high AQI days
  • Consume probiotic foods for immune support
📱
TECHNOLOGY USE
  • Set AQI alerts on AirSense & SAFAR apps
  • Check forecast before planning outdoor events
  • Use smart purifiers with auto-mode sensors
  • Install car cabin air filters (HEPA grade)
  • Track personal exposure with wearable sensors
  • Share AQI data with elderly/children in family
◈ HEALTH REMEDIES — SELECT CATEGORY
🫚
STEAM INHALATION
Add 3–4 drops of eucalyptus oil to boiling water. Inhale steam for 10 minutes twice daily. Opens blocked nasal passages, clears mucus from pollutant-irritated airways, soothes bronchial inflammation.
DAILY USE
🍯
HONEY + GINGER
Mix 1 tsp raw honey with ½ tsp ginger juice every morning. Honey coats the throat, reducing inflammation. Ginger acts as a natural bronchodilator and expectorant, helping expel trapped particulate matter.
PREVENTIVE
🌿
TULSI (HOLY BASIL)
Chew 5–6 fresh tulsi leaves or drink tulsi tea daily. Contains eugenol and rosmarinic acid — powerful anti-inflammatory phytochemicals that protect lung tissue from oxidative damage caused by PM2.5 and NO₂.
PREVENTIVE
🧄
GARLIC THERAPY
Consume 2 raw garlic cloves daily with warm water. Allicin in garlic has proven antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Supports cardiovascular health compromised by long-term pollutant exposure.
DAILY USE
🍋
JAGGERY + LEMON
Consume jaggery after exposure to dust/smoke. Jaggery acts as a lung cleanser — its iron content helps counter anemia caused by CO inhalation. Add lemon for Vitamin C-boosted antioxidant synergy.
POST-EXPOSURE
💧
SALINE NASAL RINSE
Use a neti pot with distilled saline water (¼ tsp salt in 240ml water) once daily. Physically flushes PM2.5 particles, pollen, and chemical vapors from nasal passages before they reach the lungs.
DAILY USE
💊
BRONCHODILATORS
Short-acting beta-agonists (Salbutamol) provide rapid relief during AQI spikes. Long-acting bronchodilators (Salmeterol) used for chronic conditions. Always carry rescue inhaler on high AQI days (>150).
PRESCRIPTION
🩺
N-ACETYL CYSTEINE
NAC (600mg/day) is a mucolytic and antioxidant. Boosts glutathione — the body's master antioxidant — which is depleted by air pollution exposure. Particularly effective against SO₂ and ozone damage.
CONSULT DR
💉
ANTI-INFLAMMATORY DRUGS
Corticosteroid inhalers (Beclomethasone) reduce chronic airway inflammation. Oral antihistamines help when high particulate count triggers allergic rhinitis. Never self-prescribe — see a pulmonologist.
PRESCRIPTION
🫀
CARDIAC MONITORING
PM2.5 increases risk of arrhythmia and myocardial infarction. Take Aspirin 75mg/day if cardiologically at risk and AQI consistently >150. Regular ECG monitoring recommended for elderly patients.
CONSULT DR
🧬
ANTIOXIDANT VITAMINS
Vitamin E (400 IU/day) + Vitamin C (500mg/day) + Zinc (15mg/day) create a triple-antioxidant shield. Studies show this combination reduces pollution-induced DNA damage by up to 35%.
OTC SAFE
🩻
ANNUAL SCREENINGS
Spirometry (lung function), chest X-ray, CBC (blood count), and heavy metal panel. Urban residents exposed to AQI >100 for 6+ months/year should get annual screenings starting at age 30.
ANNUAL
🌬️
HEPA AIR PURIFIERS
True HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles ≥0.3µm including PM2.5. Place units in bedroom (8 hrs of clean air during sleep) and main living area. Change filters every 6–12 months based on AQI exposure.
ESSENTIAL
🪴
INDOOR PLANTS
Spider plant removes formaldehyde. Peace lily absorbs benzene and VOCs. Aloe vera monitors air toxins. Snake plant (Sansevieria) produces O₂ at night. Areca palm humidifies and filters simultaneously.
NATURAL
🪟
VENTILATION STRATEGY
Open windows only when outdoor AQI < 50. Use mechanical ventilation with MERV-13+ filters otherwise. Maintain slight positive pressure indoors using filtered air supply to prevent outdoor air infiltration.
STRATEGY
🧹
CLEANING PRACTICES
Use HEPA vacuum cleaners — regular vacuums redistribute PM2.5 into air. Mop floors with damp cloth after dry sweeping. Avoid chemical sprays; use microfiber cloths. Change bed linens 2× weekly during poor AQI weeks.
WEEKLY
💧
HUMIDITY CONTROL
Maintain indoor humidity at 40–60%. Below 30% causes nasal dryness, making mucus barriers less effective. Above 70% promotes mold growth (another indoor pollutant). Use cool-mist humidifiers in dry seasons.
MAINTAIN
🔥
COOKING HYGIENE
Run exhaust fan during all cooking — gas stoves emit NO₂ and CO. Avoid deep frying on high AQI days. Use induction cooktops to eliminate combustion. Keep kitchen door closed from living areas when cooking.
DAILY
🫐
ANTIOXIDANT POWERFOODS
Blueberries, amla (Indian gooseberry), pomegranate — these have ORAC scores >6,000 µmol TE/100g. Anthocyanins in these fruits cross the blood-lung barrier and directly neutralize free radicals generated by PM2.5.
DAILY
🐟
OMEGA-3 FATTY ACIDS
Fatty fish (sardines, mackerel, salmon) or flaxseed oil (for vegetarians) — 2g EPA+DHA daily. Clinical studies show omega-3s reduce PM2.5-induced cardiac inflammation by 30–50%. Take with meals for absorption.
DAILY
🌾
MAGNESIUM-RICH FOODS
Spinach, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, black beans. Magnesium acts as a natural bronchodilator and anti-spasmodic for airway muscles. Pollution depletes magnesium through oxidative stress — replenishment is critical.
WEEKLY
🥦
SULFORAPHANE SOURCE
Broccoli sprouts (highest source), cauliflower, kale. Sulforaphane activates NRF2 pathway — the body's main detoxification switch. Studies from Johns Hopkins show it increases excretion of benzene and acrolein (key pollutants).
3× WEEKLY
🫖
GREEN TEA PROTOCOL
3 cups of green tea daily provides EGCG — a potent catechin that chelates heavy metals (lead, cadmium) from blood. Also reduces oxidative stress biomarkers after pollution exposure. Avoid adding milk (blocks catechins).
DAILY
🥣
GUT-LUNG AXIS DIET
Probiotics (yogurt, kefir, kimchi) strengthen the gut-lung axis. A healthy gut microbiome reduces systemic inflammation triggered by inhaled pollutants. Take probiotic supplement (10 billion CFU) on high AQI days.
HIGH AQI
🧘
PRANAYAMA BREATHING
Anulom Vilom (alternate nostril breathing) for 15 min/day increases lung capacity by 15–20% over 3 months. Bhramari (humming bee) calms vagal tone. Kapalbhati flushes stale air. Always practice indoors on AQI >100 days.
DAILY
🏊
SWIMMING
Indoor swimming in chlorinated pools is one of the safest high-intensity exercises during poor AQI. Water filters inhaled air, and the warm humid environment soothes airways. 30 min swim = 150 cal + lung conditioning.
HIGH AQI SAFE
🏋️
INDOOR STRENGTH TRAINING
Weight training and resistance bands can be done entirely indoors. Builds respiratory muscle strength (diaphragm and intercostals). Avoids the increased ventilation rate of cardio — meaning less pollutant inhalation per session.
HIGH AQI SAFE
🌅
OPTIMAL OUTDOOR TIMING
AQI is lowest between 5–7 AM when traffic is minimal and boundary layer is dispersed. Avoid 8–10 AM (rush hour), 12–3 PM (peak ozone formation from UV), and 6–9 PM (secondary particulate formation). Check forecast before going out.
TIMING
😷
MASK PROTOCOL FOR EXERCISE
N95 masks reduce PM2.5 inhalation by 95% but increase breathing resistance by 30%. Use elastomeric respirators for prolonged outdoor exercise. Never use cloth masks for exercise — insufficient filtration and cause CO₂ rebreathing.
IMPORTANT
🫧
POST-EXERCISE DETOX
After outdoor exercise on AQI>100 days: shower immediately, rinse nasal passages, gargle with turmeric salt water, drink 500ml of water with lemon. Change all clothes. This reduces secondary indoor contamination from particles on skin and hair.
POST-OUTDOOR
◈ YOUR 24-HOUR AIR QUALITY SURVIVAL GUIDE
05:00 — 07:00 · EARLY MORNING
🌄 Best window for outdoor activity
AQI is at its daily minimum. Traffic is low, boundary layer mixing is at maximum height, and overnight deposition has cleared particulates. Ideal for running, cycling, yoga outdoors.
Outdoor exercise ✓ Open windows Check AirSense forecast Pranayama practice
07:00 — 10:00 · MORNING RUSH
🚦 Traffic spike — wear N95 if commuting
Vehicle emissions peak during rush hour. NO₂ and CO spike near roads. If commuting, wear N95 mask, keep car windows closed and set AC to recirculate mode. Eat antioxidant breakfast (berries, green tea).
N95 outdoors Close car windows Avoid roadside Green tea
10:00 — 14:00 · MIDDAY
☀️ Ozone formation — sensitive groups stay indoor
UV radiation triggers photochemical reactions producing ground-level ozone (O₃). Ozone is a potent lung irritant. Sensitive groups (elderly, children, asthma patients) should remain indoors. Others limit strenuous outdoor activity.
Indoor work hours Run air purifier Hydrate well Avoid deep breathing outside
14:00 — 17:00 · AFTERNOON
🌬️ Wind helps — moderate outdoor activity OK
Afternoon convective mixing disperses pollutants to higher altitudes. Wind speeds typically peak, aiding dispersion. Light outdoor activities acceptable for healthy adults. Good time for indoor gym or swimming.
Light outdoor OK Monitor AQI app School pickup timing
17:00 — 20:00 · EVENING RUSH
🏙️ Second pollution peak — minimize outdoor exposure
Evening rush + temperature inversion = worst urban AQI period. Boundary layer collapses, trapping pollutants near ground. Cooking smoke from households also peaks. Keep windows closed. Run air purifier on high.
Windows closed Purifier on HIGH Cook with exhaust fan No outdoor jogging
20:00 — 05:00 · NIGHT
🌙 Sleep with air purifier — protect overnight
We spend 1/3 of our lives sleeping. PM2.5 penetrates deep into lungs during the 6–8 hours of rest. Run bedroom HEPA purifier all night. Keep windows closed. Change bed linens twice weekly during poor AQI periods.
HEPA purifier ON Windows closed Nasal rinse before bed Steam inhale if dry

◈ AQI-BASED ACTIVITY GUIDE
AQI 0–50 · GOOD
All outdoor activities safe. Enjoy parks, running, cycling. Keep windows open. No precautions needed.
AQI 51–100 · MODERATE
Sensitive groups limit prolonged outdoor exertion. Healthy adults: normal activities OK. Check forecast.
AQI 101–150 · UNHEALTHY FOR SENSITIVE GROUPS
Children, elderly, heart/lung patients: stay indoors. Others: wear N95 outdoors. Limit exercise to indoors.
AQI 151–200 · UNHEALTHY
Everyone should reduce outdoor activity. N95 mandatory. Run air purifier. Cancel outdoor events.
AQI 201–300 · VERY UNHEALTHY
Stay indoors. Seal doors and windows. Use purifier continuously. Emergency protocols for asthma patients.
AQI 301+ · HAZARDOUS
Health emergency. Schools/offices should close. Everyone stays indoors. Call doctor if symptoms appear.

AIRSENSE v3.0

A complete software-only air quality intelligence platform — real-time monitoring, hyper-heuristic 24h forecasting, health guidance, and actionable daily life recommendations. Built entirely in HTML, CSS & JavaScript.

🛰️
REAL-TIME DATA
Live AQI, 8 pollutants, and atmospheric data via OpenWeatherMap API. 48h history for model training. Auto-refreshes every 5 minutes.
🧠
HYPER-HEURISTIC ENGINE
7 algorithms run in parallel. Softmax-weighted ensemble selects the best blend automatically. 24-hour forecast with confidence intervals.
🛡️
PRECAUTIONS MODULE
Targeted precautions for every AQI level. Future-proofing long-term strategies. Infrastructure, health, community, and dietary advice.
💊
REMEDIES DATABASE
Evidence-based natural, medical, indoor, dietary and exercise remedies. Referenced from peer-reviewed research. Categorized by use case.
🌅
DAILY LIFE GUIDE
Hour-by-hour activity planner. AQI-indexed daily checklist. Activity safety guide across all 6 AQI categories.
⚙️
SYSTEM WORKFLOW
Complete visual workflow diagram of all 6 system layers — from data acquisition to intelligent health recommendations.
